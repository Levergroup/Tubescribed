Your best processes are probably locked inside a video somewhere.

A training call you recorded six months ago. A screen recording walkthrough you made for a new hire. A YouTube tutorial that perfectly explains how your team handles a specific task. That content exists — but it's not documented anywhere your team can actually use it.

Here's how to fix that in under 10 minutes using AI.

## Why Is Video-to-SOP a Game Changer for Teams?

Writing SOPs from scratch is slow. Most process documentation either never gets written or gets written once and immediately goes out of date.

Video content solves the knowledge capture problem — people are much more willing to record a walkthrough than write a 2,000-word document. The gap has always been in converting that video knowledge into usable written documentation.

AI closes that gap entirely.

## What Makes a Good Video for SOP Generation?

Not every video converts equally. The best source material for AI SOP generation:

- Has a clear sequential structure (step 1, step 2, step 3)
- Is under 30 minutes in length
- Has clear audio with minimal background noise
- Covers one specific process or workflow

YouTube tutorials, Loom recordings uploaded to YouTube, onboarding walkthroughs, and training session recordings all work well.

## How Do You Generate an SOP From a Video?

**Step 1 — Find or record your source video**

If you're documenting an existing process, record a 5-15 minute walkthrough explaining each step out loud. Upload it to YouTube as unlisted.

If you're using an existing YouTube tutorial your team already references — use that URL directly.

**Step 2 — Transcribe with TubeScribed**

Paste the YouTube URL into TubeScribed. The AI extracts audio, transcribes it using OpenAI Whisper, and cleans the output — removing filler words, adding punctuation, and structuring the content.

**Step 3 — Generate the SOP**

Select your brand workspace (if set up) and click Generate Output, then select SOP. TubeScribed's Claude AI layer reads the transcript and produces:

- A title and purpose statement
- A list of required tools or prerequisites
- Numbered step-by-step instructions
- Notes and warnings where relevant
- A completion checklist

**Step 4 — Review and finalize**

Add any company-specific terminology, internal system names, or approval workflows the AI couldn't know from the video alone. This typically takes under 10 minutes.

## Who Uses the Video-to-SOP Workflow?

Business coaches use it to document their methodology from coaching call recordings. Agencies use it to create client-facing process documents from tutorial videos. HR teams use it to turn training recordings into onboarding SOPs. Operations managers use it to convert tribal knowledge — the stuff that only exists in someone's head — into repeatable documented processes.

The common thread: everyone has video content that contains valuable process knowledge. Almost nobody has the time to document it manually.
